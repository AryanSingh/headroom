# Team Memory Portability

CutCtx's **Team Memory** is designed to provide outsized value by understanding the unique context of your team's code, architecture, and workflows.

We believe that your data is your data. You always have the right to export the core knowledge you have contributed to or extracted from CutCtx.

## What is Portable (Exportable)

When you use the `/v1/memory/export` endpoint, or the `export_raw()` Python API, the following fields are guaranteed to be exported:

- **Raw Content**: The actual text, code, or context snippet stored in memory.
- **Timestamps**: Creation and validity windows (`created_at`, `valid_from`, `valid_until`).
- **Provenance**: The source of the memory (e.g., whether it was manually added, extracted from a codebase scanner, or inferred from an episode).

## What is NOT Portable

CutCtx builds a proprietary intelligence layer on top of your raw memories. This layer constitutes our core technical moat and the output of our value scoring engines. The following internal mechanisms are **not** included in the export:

- **Value Scores & Relevancy Weights**: The dynamic `value_score` and `importance` weights calculated by the EWMA engine.
- **Curation Graphs & Promotion Chains**: The state transitions, approvals, and supersession links between memories.
- **Semantic Embeddings**: The vector representations generated by our embedding models.
- **Entity References**: Derived internal relationships mapping memories to specific AST nodes or repository entities.

This asymmetry ensures that while you can always take your knowledge with you, the intelligent weighting and routing that makes CutCtx's proxy unique remains protected.
