## Severity

- **Critical:** data loss, credential exposure, paid capability unavailable for
  all users, or proxy outage without a working bypass.
- **High:** one supported client or provider path fails, recovery is degraded,
  or the customer cannot trust routing or billing evidence.
- **Medium:** workaround exists and core traffic continues.
- **Low:** documentation, cosmetic, or non-blocking usability defect.

## Escalation data

Collect the Cutctx version, deployment mode, UTC timestamps, request ID, client,
provider, status code, redacted logs, recent configuration changes, and recovery
attempts. Never request a full provider key or license key in email or chat.

Critical incidents require immediate containment, a named incident owner, a
customer update cadence, and a rollback decision. Close the incident only after
the customer confirms service and the owner records follow-up work.
